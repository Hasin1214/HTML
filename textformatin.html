Cox's Bazar is the biggest sea beach in the world. Cox’s Bazar, located in southern bangladesh, is home to the world’s longest natural sea beach, stretching over 120 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal. Renowned for its golden sands, rolling waves, and picturesque sunsets, it is a popular tourist destination both locally and internationally. The beach town offers a blend of natural beauty and cultural richness, with nearby attractions like Himchari National Park, Inani Beach, and Buddhist temples in Ramu. Cox’s Bazar is also a significant hub for fishing and salt production. In recent years, it has gained global attention due to the nearby Rohingya refugee camps, highlighting both its scenic charm and humanitarian importance.
